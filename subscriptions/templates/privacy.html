Privacy

The Daily Abstract is an indie project run by one person. We respect your inbox and your privacy. Here's exactly what we collect, why, and how you control it.

What we store

  • Your email address. So we know where to send the digest. Stored encrypted at rest in a SQLite database on our server.
  • The categories and keywords you picked. So we can build your personalized digest.
  • Your IP address. Logged on subscription requests for anti-abuse only (rate limit + audit log). Not linked to your identity beyond that.
  • What papers we sent you. So we don't send the same paper twice.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell, rent, or share your email with anyone.
  • We don't track you on third-party sites.
  • We don't use analytics (no Google Analytics, no Plausible, nothing).
  • We don't profile you for ads. There are no ads.

Third parties we rely on

  • Brevo — sends the actual emails. They see your email address but only act as the delivery transport.
  • Mailsac — receives the inbox where your replies arrive. So we can map "reply with 1 3 5" to PDFs.
  • Let's Encrypt — issues our HTTPS certificate.

All three are industry-standard providers with their own privacy policies. We don't share data with them beyond what's strictly necessary for delivery.

How to delete your data

Click the unsubscribe link in any digest. Your subscription is marked as cancelled immediately. To request full deletion of all records (including audit logs), email the address in the footer.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change how we handle data we'll update this page and notify active subscribers by email at least 30 days before any change takes effect.

Contact

For privacy questions or data requests, write to the contact in the footer or open an issue at our GitHub repo.
